Overview

This project utilizes the public Medicare Part D prescriber files (2018-2023), NPPES data, and public CMS Open Payment files to explore whether advertising payments for JAK Inhibitor medication (Xeljanz, Rinvoq, etc.) are associated with JAK prescribing volume, patient volume, and if there are differences in prescriber characteristics. I utilize R to filter the Part D files to find NPIs that have prescribed JAK inhibitors at least once from 2018-2023. I then connect the NPPES dataset to find NPI charcteristics like gender, zipcode of practice, and enumeration date. Next I connect the Open Payments dataset to determine which of these JAK prescribers received a drug advertising payment from a JAK manufacturer. I then developed the above outputs that show that paid JAK prescribers supply, on average, 10 times more JAK medication, and have over triple the number of JAK patients.
